## Task 1 `mingo03` noise in `raw_tt=34` is channel talking / electronic noise
---

### Figure
![Task 1 noise in mingo03](FIGURES/2026_04_20_noise_in_mingo03.png)

### Observation
The filtered Task 1 `T` vs `Q` scatter for `mingo03`, with `raw_tt=34` and event timestamp `2025-07-23 15:15:12`, shows a clear abnormal population.

In this snapshot the effect is most visible in planes `3` and `4`, where several panels show narrow charge clustering together with displaced or broadened time bands that do not look like the cleaner populations seen in the upper planes.

The most remarkable feature in this example is that many rejected points lying outside the coincidence window still carry high charge. That is not the usual pattern. Normally, when a point falls outside the coincidence window, it tends to have very low charge and is easy to read as a small accidental or a weak noise population. Here that is not what happens: the out-of-window population remains charge-rich.

### Interpretation
The current reading of this figure is that the effect is **not** a trigger-topology issue.

Even though the selected population is `raw_tt=34`, the figure should not be interpreted as "a problem caused by the trigger requiring planes 3 and 4". The more plausible explanation is channel-to-channel talking and general electronic noise or pickup that contaminates the measured strip times and charges.

So, in this case the noisy population happens to be visible in two planes at the same time, but that does not make it a specifically two-plane effect. The same electronics problem could in principle appear in other plane combinations as well.

This also makes the rejected population useful in a second way: these out-of-coincidence, high-charge points can be used as a concrete example of self-trigger charge. In other words, they are not only evidence of timing incompatibility with the coincidence window, but also a good visual reference for the kind of charge response that can survive outside the accepted coincidence selection.

### Why this matters
This shifts the interpretation upstream:

- the key diagnostic is the structured `T`-vs-`Q` pattern itself;
- `raw_tt=34` is only the subset where the effect is especially clear in this example;
- the unusual part is not only that the points are rejected in time, but that they keep large charge while being rejected;
- that makes the rejected cloud a useful example population for self-trigger charge studies;
- the problem belongs to Task 1 electronics-quality monitoring, not only to trigger-type studies.
