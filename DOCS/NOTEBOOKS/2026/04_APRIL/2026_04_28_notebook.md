## HV-down plus AC-on regime suggests the correction is clean when streamers disappear
---

### Figures
![Observed and corrected real-data rate](FIGURES/2026_04_28_step5_real_rate_correction.png)

![Original and corrected rate vs empirical efficiencies](FIGURES/2026_04_28_step5_real_rate_vs_efficiencies_2x2.png)

### Observation
Using `ANOTHER_METHOD` in the configuration branch with:

- `metadata_source = robust_efficiency`
- `rate_family = four_plane` / `rate_1234_hz`
- `selected_feature_columns_mode = minimal_empirical`

the correction appears to work very well in the period where the HV is down and the AC is on.

This is important because that regime looks like the streamer-free limit. Once the streamers practically disappear, the corrected rate returns to its usual level and the corrected rate-versus-efficiency relation becomes flat. In other words, even in this "pure" configuration, without additional tuning tricks, the method behaves correctly there.

### Interpretation
I still suspect that there is residual noise in the general case.

The reason is precisely that the clean control period behaves so well. If the method becomes essentially perfect when the HV is reduced and the AC is on, then the remaining imperfections outside that regime are likely not coming from the basic correction logic alone. They are more consistent with extra contamination still present in the data, probably related to streamers or to some remaining noisy behavior that is not fully encoded in the efficiency variables.

So the current reading is:

- when streamers disappear, the correction is excellent;
- when streamer activity is present, there is still extra structure that the present correction does not absorb completely;
- this makes the streamer-rich periods look like the right place to keep searching for residual noise or missing variables.

### Why this matters
This is a useful control result.

It means the correction framework is not failing everywhere. On the contrary, in the HV-down plus AC-on regime it behaves exactly as desired. That makes the remaining discrepancies much more informative, because they now point to a specific physical or instrumental ingredient still missing from the model rather than to a completely broken correction method.
